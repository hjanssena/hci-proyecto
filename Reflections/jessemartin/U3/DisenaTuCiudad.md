One of the main issues I encountered during my time as a volunteer in Diseña Tu Ciudad had to do with the accesibility attribute. I encountered people who couldn't read very well either because the letter size
was too small, had reading issues or similar. I noticed that people couldn't recognize the proposals from the address alone, so some research could be done to incluide visual aids like images, text-to-speech capabilities
or bigger lettering options. There was even a deaf lady who probably had a hard time reading the proposals, I'm not sure because I couldn't really ask her.

Most of these issues occured during the in site registration, with the exception of some problems in the online vote platform that took a small amount of people to prefer going to the votation sites.

In all of these cases there was a level of assistance required and most of these were resolved after some brief explaining.

In general there weren't any complaints at all, I can attribute this to the time required to register and vote being relatively quick compared to similar processes like federal voting.
