Reflection #1 - Using Personas and it's benefits

According to Lene Nielsen **\[1]**, there is no consensus on what is the main benefit of using the persona method. In our case, it helped us focus on the main usability attributes we should work on based on the persona we created. It also allowed us to know what not to work on since the persona most likely won’t need a certain usability aspect.

In our project the persona is fairly adept at using a computer and values efficiency a lot, so our decision was to focus a bit less on the learnability and ease of use attribute and more on the efficiency side of things. We decided that making the use of the system easy and fast was the right path since the user will be using it a lot in the long term, and having a learning curve will be a one time event that wont be much of a problem due to them already being proficient using a computer.

In our case, creating a persona flashed a light on the attributes we should pay attention to, but maybe in other cases, in projects that have way more reach and different kinds of users, the persona method may be harder to implement since reconciling many user’s need is a very hard and complex task.

\[1] L. Nielsen, "Personas," in _The Encyclopedia of Human-Computer Interaction_, 2nd ed., M. Soegaard and R. F. Dam, Eds. Aarhus, Denmark: Interaction Design Foundation, 2014. \[Online]. Available: <https://ixdf.org/literature/book/the-encyclopedia-of-human-computer-interaction-2nd-ed/personas>
